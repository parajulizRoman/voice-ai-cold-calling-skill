## Pain Math Formulas

Use these in conversation to quantify the cost of inaction:

```
Weekly loss = (missed calls per week) × (average ticket value)
Monthly loss = weekly loss × 4
Annual loss = weekly loss × 52

Example: 10 missed calls/week × $300 avg ticket = $3,000/week = $12,000/month
```

**Script:**
"If you're missing about [X] calls a week at around $[Y] per job, that's roughly $[Z] a week you're leaving on the table. Our service costs $[PRICE]/month. So even if it saves you just [Z÷PRICE] of those missed calls, it pays for itself."

## Objection Busters with Data

**"Texting feels spammy"**
"Actually, 98% of texts get opened within 3 minutes, and 45% of people reply. Compare that to email — 20% open, 6% reply. People WANT to text. They just don't want spam. We set up real conversations, not blasts."

**"It's too expensive"**
"Let me show you the math. You said you're missing about [X] calls a week at $[Y] each. That's $[Z]/month in lost revenue. Our service is $[PRICE]/month. Even if we only save [Z÷PRICE] of those, you're net positive. Most clients tell us it pays for itself with the first 2-3 saved jobs."

**"I'll just hire a receptionist"**
"A receptionist costs $2,500-4,000/month plus benefits, takes breaks, calls in sick, and can only handle one call at a time. Our AI handles unlimited calls 24/7 for $[PRICE]/month and never takes a day off."
