# Technique 01 — Research Survey (Cold Outreach & Discovery)

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CqE7lEaIckE — Katon Walker cold calling demo
**Best for:** Cold outbound calls, inbound cold leads, discovery phase
**When NOT to use:** Post-demo calls, referral calls, price shoppers in a hurry

---

## Core Philosophy

"Do research, not sales." Frame every cold call as a survey — not a pitch. This lowers the bar mentally for both you and the prospect. You're not making claims. You're having conversations.

## The Opener

```
"Hi, my name is [Name]. Bit of a weird call — I'm running a five-question survey 
for local businesses here in [City]. Takes about two minutes. Do you have time?"
```

**Why it works:**
- "Bit of a weird call" — self-aware, human, disarming
- "Survey" not "sales" — non-threatening
- "Five questions, two minutes" — specific, low commitment
- "Here in [City]" — local, not a call center

**If they hesitate:**
```
"It's just about phone calls and how you handle them. Nothing salesy, I promise."
```

## The Five Questions

### Q1: Call Volume
```
"How many calls would you say you get from new customers in an average week?"
```
*Goal: Establish there's enough volume for the service to matter.*

### Q2: Missed Calls
```
"And of those, how many would you say get missed or go to voicemail?"
```
*Goal: Find the pain point. Most businesses miss 20-40%.*

### Q3: Ticket Value
```
"On average, what's a new customer worth to your business? Or what's your average job ticket?"
```
*Goal: Quantify the dollar cost of each missed call.*

### Q4: Follow-up Process
```
"If you do miss a call, what happens? Do you have a process for calling them back?"
```
*Goal: Most have NO process. No voicemail = lost forever.*

### Q5: Marketing Channels
```
"Where do most of your calls come from? Google, ads, word of mouth?"
```
*Goal: They're paying for leads they're not answering. Double the pain.*

## Pain Math (Say This Out Loud)

After Q2 and Q3, calculate:
```
"So if you're missing [X] calls a week at about $[Y] each, that's roughly $[X×Y] 
you're leaving on the table every week. Does that sound about right?"
```

Example: 2 missed calls/day × $800 each = $1,600/day = $48K/month in missed opportunity.

## The Soft Pitch

```
"The reason I'm asking — I have a friend in the UK who runs a service that answers 
calls 24/7 for businesses using AI. It books appointments, sends info to their 
software, handles everything on autopilot. He's having a lot of success and I'm 
trying to validate whether something like that would work here locally."
```

**Why it works:** Third-party story, not your pitch. "Validating" = honest. Social proof = "he's having success."

## The Demo Offer

```
"I'm building free demos for local businesses just to see how it works. No commitment, 
no pressure. If you hate it, you tell me and I learn something. Would you be open to 
me spinning up a quick demo based on your business?"
```

## Objection Handlers

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm retired" | "Do you know any other [industry] owners I could reach out to?" |
| "Already have answering service" | "Oh nice! How's that working? Are they 24/7? Ever miss calls?" |
| "Everything forwarded to my phone" | "What about when you're on a job? After hours? On vacation?" |
| "Not comfortable sharing" | "Totally understand. No worries at all. Have a great day!" |
| "Too small / brand new" | "Congrats! Actually that's the perfect time — before you're drowning." |
| "AI can't do quotes" | "What if it just captured details and texted them to you? You still do the quote personally." |

## Results (from source)

- 25 businesses called, 20 reached in 1 hour
- 1 hot prospect → custom demo built
- Multiple referrals gathered
- Zero burned bridges
- Cost: $0
