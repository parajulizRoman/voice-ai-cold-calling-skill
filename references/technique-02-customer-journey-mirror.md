# Technique 02 — Customer Journey Mirror (Closing After Demo)

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FCrJoBjya2c — Katon Walker closing demo
**Best for:** Post-demo calls, warm leads who've seen the product, AI Impact Assessment calls
**When NOT to use:** Cold calls (use Technique 01), price-first callers, very technical prospects, calls under 3 minutes

---

## Core Philosophy

**"You don't want to sell them on why they should use it. You want them to personally identify with their customers' journey — so they have the epiphany themselves."**

The demo already showed them the solution on their own site. Your job on this call is to lead them to their own "aha moment." You're not pitching — you're guiding a conversation where they arrive at the conclusion.

## The Five-Step Mirror

### Step 1: Where do you search?
```
"So when you need a service — say a plumber or electrician — where do you usually search?"
```
*They'll say Google.*
```
"Yep, most of us do."
```

### Step 2: Do you scroll?
```
"When you search on Google, do you ever go past the second page?"
```
*They'll say no.*
```
"Yeah, makes sense. Most of us are the same way."
```

### Step 3: How do you choose?
```
"So when you're on the first page, how do you determine who to reach out to?"
```
*They'll say: top result, best reviews, most reviews.*
```
"Yeah, most everyone does the same."
```

### Step 4: How many do you call?
```
"Do you usually only call one business?"
```
*They'll say: no, two or three.*
```
"Got it. So let's say you call the first one and they don't answer. What do you do?"
```
*They'll say: call the next one.*
```
"And if that one doesn't answer either?"
```
*They'll say: call the next.*
```
"So basically, you go with whoever picks up and helps you move forward."
```

### Step 5: The Mirror + Pain Lock + Close
```
"So, would it be fair to say that YOUR customers probably behave in that same exact way?"
```
*They'll agree.*
```
"And would you agree that if your business isn't engaging leads the right way — 
whether by phone, chat, or text — those leads are probably bouncing to your competitors?"
```
*They'll agree again.*
```
"So let me ask you — can you see any reason why you wouldn't want to implement this 
that closes the gap and makes you never miss a lead again?"
```

**That's the close.** Don't speak after this. Let them sit with it.

## Response Handling

**If they say "no reason":**
```
"That's what we thought. Let me walk you through what getting set up looks like."
```
→ Move to pricing.

**If they hesitate:**
```
"What's holding you back? Is it the price, the setup, or something else?"
```
→ Address the specific concern.

**If they bring up price before the mirror is done:**
```
"We'll get to that — but first, let me ask you something so I can make sure this 
is actually the right fit for you."
```
→ Continue the mirror.

## Pricing Reveal

**Only reveal pricing AFTER they're bought in on the problem.** Never before.

```
"The full package is $297 to set up and $197 a month — no contracts, cancel anytime. 
That covers the AI answering every call 24/7, automated review requests after every job, 
and instant text-back for any missed calls. Most of our clients tell us it pays for 
itself with the first two or three saved jobs."
```

**If they push back on price:**
```
"I get it. Let me ask — if this saves you even 3-4 missed calls a month at your average 
ticket price, what's that worth to you?"
```
→ Let them do the math. It'll be higher than $197.

## Why This Works

1. **It's their logic, not yours.** Every answer comes from them. You only ask questions.
2. **Universal behavior.** Everyone Googles, checks reviews, calls the top results, picks whoever answers. Undeniable.
3. **The mirror creates empathy.** "My customers do exactly what I do."
4. **They quantify their own pain.** They admit leads bounce to competitors — you didn't say it.
5. **The close is a question, not a pitch.** "Can you see any reason why you wouldn't..." — flips the burden. Now THEY justify NOT using it.

## When NOT to Use

- **Technical prospect**: They'll debate the analogy instead of internalizing it
- **Already has AI service**: Pivot to "How's it performing? Any gaps?"
- **Enterprise/long sales cycle**: Mirror doesn't map to complex B2B buying
- **Under 3 minutes**: Not enough time for all 5 steps
- **Price-first caller**: They want numbers, not a journey — use Technique 04 instead

## Full Pipeline

```
TECHNIQUE 01 (Cold Call)
  → Research survey → name the pain → soft pitch → free demo
  
BUILD DEMO (10 min offline)
  → Personalized to THEIR business on mock site
  → AI voice/chat answering real questions about their services
  
TECHNIQUE 02 (Close Call)
  → Customer Journey Mirror → "aha moment" → close question → pricing
```
